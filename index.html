FACTAGE - Fairer Active Ageing for Europe

Exploring emerging inequalities associated with longer working lives.
A Joint Programming Initiative - More Years Better Lives (MYBL) - project.

BACKGROUND

Population ageing challenges the way welfare states are organised.

The FACTAGE project starts from the premise that there is substantial scope for increasing the length of average working lives and active ageing. However, careful attention must be paid to the issue of equality concerning the elderly, not least prospectively.

Download the flyer

OUR RESEARCH

Key research questions center around the impact of longer working lives on inequalities related to:
• the labour market
• (healthy) live expectancies
• subjective well-being
Which policies can best promote fairness within and between generations?

NEW FACTAGE Book

Policies for an Ageing Workforce: Work-life balance, working conditions and equal opportunities

Extending average working lives by 10 years, while ensuring an adequate social safety net for those unable to work into their late 60s and 70s, is a major social policy challenge today and for the coming decades. Tackling this challenge involves delving into policy areas that range from working conditions, skills and lifelong learning, pensions, socio-economic inequalities in health and life expectancy to the design of a much broader agenda on active ageing.

FACTAGE-CEPS event: Ageing, health and well-being

On 25 June, CEPS hosted the FACTAGE conference Ageing, health and well-being. There is a broad consensus that a key response to demographic ageing is to extend the length of working lives. There is much less agreement on the central questions of how and for whom to extend the number of years in employment.

FACTAGE Training on Differential Mortality Estimation from EU-SILC Longitudinal Data
